Methodology

Prices are modeled as price(t) = a + b·t + c₁sin(ωt) + c₂cos(ωt) + c₃sin(2ωt) + c₄cos(2ωt), fit by ordinary least squares on 48 month-end observations, where t is days since the first record and ω = 2π / 365.25. In-sample dates are answered by direct linear interpolation between the two nearest real observations; only dates beyond the historical record fall back to the fitted curve.

01 / TREND A persistent multi-year increase in average price, driven by the linear term.
02 / SEASONALITY A repeating annual cycle — winter heating demand pushes prices up, summer eases them down.
03 / HORIZON Extrapolation is capped at one year past the last observation, beyond which model confidence is not assumed.
